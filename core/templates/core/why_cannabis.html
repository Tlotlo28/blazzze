WHY WEED? 🌿

Real talk: people have used cannabis for thousands of years for a bunch of different reasons. The science is catching up. Here's what research suggests, what the types actually mean, and what Blazzze refuses to lie to you about.

WHAT THE RESEARCH SUGGESTS

Sleep

Many users report cannabis helps them fall asleep faster, especially indica-dominant strains. CBD is also being studied for sleep disorders.

Heavy regular use may affect REM sleep quality long-term.

Pain

Research suggests cannabinoids may help with chronic pain — particularly neuropathic pain that doesn't respond well to standard meds.

Not a replacement for prescribed treatment. Talk to a doctor.

Anxiety

Mixed evidence. Low doses of THC and CBD may reduce anxiety in some users. High doses can increase it. Highly individual.

If you have an anxiety disorder, start low and slow — or skip entirely.

Appetite & Nausea

Well-documented. THC is studied and used clinically for chemotherapy-induced nausea and appetite loss.

aka how the munchies became a medical term.

Inflammation

Cannabinoids show anti-inflammatory properties in laboratory and animal studies. Human research is ongoing.

Topicals (creams) are an interesting area to watch.

Creativity & Mood

Anecdotal but widely reported — particularly with sativa-dominant strains. Hard to measure scientifically, easy to enjoy.

Your "amazing idea at 2am" still needs sober review at 11am.

TYPES AT A GLANCE

INDICA

Body high. Couch, blanket, deep sleep. The "I'll get up in a minute" energy. Better for nighttime.

SATIVA

Head high. Energetic, talkative, sometimes giggly. Better for daytime, social settings, creative tasks.

HYBRID

Both. Most modern strains are hybrids — bred for specific balances of indica/sativa traits. Read the label.

  • Claim cannabis cures anything. Research is ongoing.
  • Replace your doctor. Talk to one for medical questions.
  • Pretend it's risk-free. It isn't. Dependency, anxiety, and impaired driving are real.
  • Tell you what dose is right for you. Start low, go slow.
  • Endorse use by minors, pregnant people, or anyone with relevant medical contraindications.
